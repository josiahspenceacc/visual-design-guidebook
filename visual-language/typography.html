  • Basic anatomy and broad classifications
  • Weights and styles
  • Things to consider when choosing typefaces:
    • Body versus display
      • Make sure you use only highly readable typefaces for body copy
    • What the typeface communicates / is it appropriate
    • Limit to 2 typefaces in general
    • How many weights and styles are available
  • Tips for readable type (especially for body copy):
    • 16px minimum body size
    • Make sure body copy line length is 45-75 characters
    • Use sufficient contrast (by having different lightness)
    • Avoid pure black on pure white in most cases
    • Try body copy leading of 1.5 times the size of the text and adjust from there
  • Hierarchy:
    • What happens when everything is trying to be important (when everything is important, nothing is important)
    • Flat hierarchy
    • Spacing
    • Color
    • Weight
    • Italics
    • Size
    • Typeface
    • Decoration
    • Case
    • Combinations
    • Golden rule: The thing that is different stands out as important
  • Typographic scales:
    • Use a consistent body copy size throughout the page
    • Use a consistent scale and styles for headings
  • Make things scannable by including lots of headings and visual anchors and keeping the text in small digestible chunks
  • Mixing left aligned and center aligned text is always tricky. I recommend matching them.
